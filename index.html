Hi, I'm Dock Hunter. A Web Developer.

I've been watching the cryptospace around 5 years, but after my first foray into cryptotrading went sideways I chose to watch the new technologies develop. What's taken traditional finance centuries to develop has almost fully formed in crypto in less than 15 years, along with new niches not possible without cryptographic techniques such as flash loans. After watching a halvening cycle and seeing some bigger trends I felt confident to trade. Now after seeing the gas-crisis and resulting L2s, the emergence of collateralized stablecoins, early DeFi, NFTs becoming mainstream, and now the split sentiment of an accumulation phase I see it's time to get into the dev side of the space.

Information About me

In highschool I realized how enjoyable engineering could be and ended up on projects ranging from building a battery-powered, 3 wheeled, race tricycle to failing to code an algorithmic cryptotrader on Poloniex (in early 2015, if only it'd worked). These interests led me to Auburn University where I found the linux and security side of computer science intriguing enough to swap majors from chemical to software engineering. Four years after I got there it was time for me to move on, and ever since I've been trading.
Outside of screens, I try to spend my time in the woods, or at least active. I hang my hat around north Alabama which puts plenty of mountains, forests, and bodies of water within a day's drive. I enjoy volunteering at a local homeless shelter where I solve any IT issues and occasionally help put up drywall, and 'volunteer' is too formal a word but I'll help out at the farmers co-op if anyone needs it.
